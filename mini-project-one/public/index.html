Malted Barley

Malted Barley generally makes up for all of the fermentable sugars in the wort.

Hops

Hops are responsible for bitter and fruity flavours in the beer, depending on when they are added to the boil .

Yeast

Yeast is responsible for consuming sugars and converting them in ethanol (alcohol)

Brew Kettle

The malt if added to the kettle and typically left to sit at 65 - 68 degrees celsius for 60 minutes, the malt is then removed and the wort is boiled for an 60 minutes.

Fermenter

The wort is chilled to around 20 degrees celsius and then added to the fermenter along with yeast and left to ferment for a couple of weeks

Corny Keg

Once the beer is fully fermented it is then transferred to a keg, carbonated, and chilled for serving.
